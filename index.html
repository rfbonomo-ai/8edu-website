Common Questions

Do you need a high school diploma to be admitted into a university?

No. We've successfully placed students at Russell Group universities in the UK, leading American universities, and English-taught bachelor's programs across Europe (Holland, Italy, Spain, France, Germany, Czech Republic, Poland).

Major universities actively welcome homeschooled students with strong A-Level results. Research shows homeschoolers often score higher on standardized tests and achieve competitive admission rates.

What about supervision during the day?

This program isn't for students who need constant supervision—it's for students who are ready for more.

Our students are academically prepared (A's and B's on IGCSEs) and personally mature. They manage their time, complete their work, and take responsibility.

This is actually an advantage: Your child learns independence while still living at home. It's the perfect transition before university—when they'll have complete freedom anyway.

If your child isn't ready for self-directed study, they're not ready for this program.

Isn't online learning inferior to in-person?

Not when done right.

Our model isn't "watch videos and figure it out yourself." It's 18 hours per week of live, interactive instruction with expert teachers in small cohorts of 8 students.

We also embrace the latest learning tools—AI study assistants, interactive notebooks, adaptive platforms—tools that traditional schools ban but that students will use throughout their careers. We don't fear technology; we teach students to master it.

The result? No time wasted on classroom management, no pace held back by unmotivated students, no commuting. Just focused teaching with students who are ready.

We proved this works last year—our students are now at university.
